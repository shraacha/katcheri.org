Pasupathi sir is a product of Kalakshetra, Chennai, India and learnt music under Sri. �Tiger� Varadachariar,
Sri. Budalur Krishnamurthy Sastrigal, Sri. T.K. Ramaswamy Iyengar, Sri. Mudikondan Venkatarama Iyer and Sri.
Mysore Vasudeva Acharya. He also learnt to play the veena under Smt. Kalpagam Swaminathan apart from being
proficient in bharatanatyam and nattuvangam.
He graduated with a post graduate diploma in Music from Kalakshetra and went on to serve on its faculty before
leaving for Tirupathi. He then joined S.V. Music College Tirupahti as a Lecturer in 1960 and retired in 1987
as Principal of the institution.
He cam back to Kalkshetra to serve as Vice Principal as per his promise to Smt. Rukmani Devi who brought him up,
till his retirement in 1995.
He has composed music for several Annamacharya Sankeerthanas and also composed the music for the Kalakshetra
dance drama production of �Panchali Shabadam� and "Thirukural Bhatam" presented by Dhananjayans.
He is a A-Top Artiste for All-India Radio and has released audio cassettes entitled �Vande Vasudevam�
(Mysore Vasudeva Acharya compositions) and a dual-set of Annamacharya Sankeerthanas - Annamaya pada kaumudi
and Padaparijatam relased by TTD.

Meaning:
This pancharathna krithi has been well set on the easiest of ragas, Arabhi. This Krithi has been carved out in
a language full of liberty, teasing tone, metaphor and simile without having a surfeit of adjectives � all the
while arresting the attention of the singers. Thyagaraja swamigal, in this krithi, appeared to be telling the
greatness of the lord in a lucid manner most enthusiastically. The style adopted in this krithi is very sweet
in comparison to the other four kirthanas. Oh Lord you are an opportunist, You deceived your parents Devaki ,
Vasudeva as also the gopikas who surrendered to you, You mischievously smile when Yashoda innocently folded you
with love at the thought that she too would be disappointed on being separated from him, You falsified the
aforesaid words of Sruti and Smruti. You have somehow not come to me despite the fact that I was overjoyed
in keeping your memory always in my heart, You preached patience, tolerance in the face of adversity freedom
from anger, satsang etc; and coolly accepted my pujas, You give bhakti and peace, Despite all this you
have steadfastly not come closer to me to the end, Thus this kirthana, a gem amongst the five kirthanas
which bring out the thought and reminiscences of Shri Thayagaraja; is a great gift to the singer and bhaktas
