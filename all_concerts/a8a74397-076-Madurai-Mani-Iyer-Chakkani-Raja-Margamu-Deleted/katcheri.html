Meaning:

Notes:

Tyagaraja presents a picture of Rama's domestic life after his return to Ayodhya. Playing a game of pallanguzhi, with Sita, he relaxes after a day's work as King. He wins the game and enjoys his banter with Sita. Hanuman and Bharata, are delighted to listen to the talk of their Lord and His wife.

The line A bhAvamErigi is not present in Kirtanacharya CR Srinivasa Iyengar's version of the kriti. He points out in his 1930 writings, that this line did not exist in the KV Srinivasa Iyengar's Sangita Ratnavali in its 1916 edition which is as per the Umayalpuram school, as evinced by the 1908 Dowden and Co publication, of Narasimha Bhagavatar. Subsequently, he points out, KV Srinivasa Iyengar in his 1916 Tamil edition of Tyagaraja kritis, has included the line. The Kirtanacharya points out that subsequent editions and later compilations of Tyagaraja kritis include this line, but that he is not aware of the varnamETTu for the line. The peculiarity of the song is that it gets over in seven AvarttAs of tALam without this line, which is contrary to the practice of Tyagaraja (though some variations do exist). This line may have been added by KV Srinivasa Iyengar to satisfy the condition of eight AvarttAs

The game of pallanguzhi is played in South India and mainly by women. Rama, entertains his wife in her domain and wins in it too. The raga, pratApa varALi is a rare one and in the pallavi of the song itself, the bard manages to fit in the complete ArOhaNam and avarOhaNam as though to make the structure clear. The word pratApa means valour, yet as a contrast, this kriti shows none of Rama's valour, but paints him in domestic colours. The late Madurai Mani Iyer made this kriti very popular.
