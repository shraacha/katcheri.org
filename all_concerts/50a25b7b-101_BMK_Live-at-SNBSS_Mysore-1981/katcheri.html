  • on Nov 6, 2012, 9:16pm GMT said:

    Sri Jayram Sataluri's review:

    This is truly one of the greatest BMK concerts of all time. I had been looking for the whole concert since listening to Balagopala and Evarimata and I was not disappointed. The accompaniments are in top form here.

    It seems like this concert took place during Gokulashtami or Sri Krishna Janmashtami as most of the krithis are dedicated to Lord Sri Krishna. The start of the concert begins with a bang with his varnam and Sadinchene. One of my favorite compositions written by him is Ganasudharasame and it is part of the Raganga Ravali Melakartha krithis. This version is amazing and reminds me of the commercial version he released in 1990. Balagopala and Chalu Chalu are great middle pieces for the concerts. I have been looking for a great live version of Chalu Chalu and I found it. I like his Hari at the end of the song. This segways into Evarimata, the main piece and it is outstanding. The alapana is brilliant and Annavarapu's response is sublime. But it is the krithi that stands out as he really puts a lot of soul into it; it seems as if Sri Thyagaraja himself is singing to us. I don't think there have been any standout versions of Evarimata like this. The only version that comes close is by his own guru, Parupalli Ramakrishnayya Panthulu. The elaborations of "shaktigala mahadevudavivani" are great and the swarams bring the rendition to another level. I especially liked the interplay between the three performers during the swarams; Balamurali would sing, the violin would repeat it and the mridangam would finish it off. The Thani avarthanam by Sri Umayalpuram K Sivaraman is out of this world and I liked the part when we could hear Balamurali sing Pa Da Sa during the softer parts. The ending of the Thani in tisra gathi is simply mindblowing. The rest of the krithis are excellent especially Yenu Madidarenu, Krishnam Kalayasakhi, Krishna Yanabarade, Yamihe, Kadanakuthuhalam and Nagumomu and they provide a fitting end.

    Overall, this BMK concert is outstanding and one will greatly appreciate his genius as well as the accompaniments' performances throughout.
